Starting a **cybersecurity company that provides VAPT (Vulnerability Assessment & Penetration Testing)** across **Web, Android, Cloud, IoT, and AI/ML** is absolutely doable—but it needs a **clear roadmap**, not just technical skills. Below is a **practical, execution-focused guide** used by real security consultancies.

---

## 1. Define Your Company Clearly (Most People Skip This)

Before tools or branding, decide **exactly what you sell**.

### Core Services (Start Narrow, Expand Later)

**Phase 1 (recommended):**

- Web Application VAPT
    
- Android Application VAPT
    
- Cloud Security Review (AWS / Azure / GCP)
    

**Phase 2:**

- IoT Security Testing
    
- Network & Infrastructure Pentesting
    
- Red Teaming
    

**Phase 3 (advanced):**

- AI/ML Model Security
    
- LLM Prompt Injection & Model Abuse Testing
    
- Adversarial ML
    

👉 Trying to launch _everything at once_ usually fails.

---

## 2. Legal & Business Setup (Non-Negotiable)

### Register the company

Choose one:

- Sole Proprietorship (fastest)
    
- LLP / Private Limited (better for clients & contracts)
    

### Mandatory documents

- Business registration
    
- GST / Tax ID (country-specific)
    
- Company bank account
    
- NDA & Authorization templates
    
- Cyber liability insurance (optional but recommended)
    

⚠️ **Never test without written authorization** (very important legally).

---

## 3. Build Your Technical Capability Stack

You need **repeatable, professional testing**, not ad-hoc hacking.

### Web VAPT

- Standards: OWASP Top 10, ASVS
    
- Tools: Burp Suite Pro, OWASP ZAP, Nuclei
    
- Skills: auth bypass, IDOR, business logic flaws
    

### Android VAPT

- Tools: MobSF, JADX, Frida, Drozer
    
- Skills: SSL pinning bypass, insecure storage, API abuse
    

### Cloud Security

- Standards: CIS Benchmarks
    
- Tools: ScoutSuite, Prowler, CloudSploit
    
- Skills: IAM misconfig, S3 exposure, privilege escalation
    

### IoT Security

- Firmware analysis (binwalk)
    
- Hardware interfaces (UART, JTAG)
    
- Network protocol testing (MQTT, CoAP)
    

### AI/ML Security (high demand, low competition)

- Model extraction
    
- Prompt injection
    
- Data poisoning
    
- Inference abuse
    

---

## 4. Create Professional Deliverables (This Wins Clients)

Clients pay for **reports**, not exploits.

### Every VAPT report must include:

- Executive summary (non-technical)
    
- Risk scoring (CVSS)
    
- Proof of Concept
    
- Impact analysis
    
- Clear remediation steps
    
- Compliance mapping (ISO, SOC2, PCI DSS)
    

📌 Poor reports = no repeat business.

---

## 5. Certifications That Actually Matter

### For credibility (pick 1–2 first):

- OSCP / PNPT (pentesting credibility)
    
- CEH (marketing-friendly)
    
- AWS Security Specialty (cloud trust)
    
- ISO 27001 Lead Auditor (enterprise clients)
    

Company-level:

- ISO 27001 (huge trust booster)
    
- SOC 2 readiness (optional)
    

---

## 6. Pricing Model (Simple & Realistic)

### Common pricing (example ranges)

- Web VAPT: $1,000 – $5,000
    
- Android VAPT: $1,500 – $6,000
    
- Cloud Review: $2,000 – $10,000
    
- IoT Testing: $5,000+
    
- AI/ML Security: Premium pricing (few competitors)
    

### Pricing strategies

- Per-asset
    
- Per-scope
    
- Retainer model (monthly recurring revenue)
    

💡 Start slightly under market → raise prices after testimonials.

---

## 7. Getting Your First Clients (Critical Part)

### Best channels for early traction

- LinkedIn (daily security posts + case studies)
    
- Cold emails to startups & SaaS founders
    
- Bug bounty profiles (credibility)
    
- Partnerships with dev agencies & MSPs
    
- Free initial risk assessment (lead magnet)
    

### What clients want to hear

❌ “We hack systems”  
✅ “We reduce breach risk and help you pass audits”

---

## 8. Build a Small, Strong Team

Initial roles:

- Lead Pentester
    
- Cloud Security Specialist
    
- Report Writer / GRC analyst
    
- Sales / Business development
    

Avoid hiring juniors too early.

---

## 9. Legal & Ethical Safeguards

- Signed Authorization Letter (ALWAYS)
    
- NDA + Scope document
    
- Logging & evidence handling
    
- Responsible disclosure policy
    

🚨 Unauthorized testing = criminal offense.

---

## 10. Scale Smartly

After 6–12 months:

- Productized VAPT packages
    
- Automated scanning + manual validation
    
- Offer compliance services (ISO, SOC2, HIPAA)
    
- SaaS security monitoring
    

---

## 11. Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Selling “cheap hacking”  
❌ No contract or authorization  
❌ Over-promising coverage  
❌ Copy-paste reports  
❌ Ignoring business development

---

## Final Advice

A successful VAPT company is:

- **40% technical skill**
    
- **40% communication & trust**
    
- **20% branding & process**
